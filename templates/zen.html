Sosoku-Kan is a fundamental Zazen practice where the practitioner focuses on counting their breaths. It is considered particularly helpful for beginners and is a standard practice in Zen meditation.

Start by taking a few deep breaths to settle into your posture. Then, breathe naturally through your nose, and begin counting each exhalation from one to ten. When you reach ten, start again at one. If you lose count or become distracted, gently bring your attention back to the breath and start over at one. The aim is to cultivate mindfulness and concentration by focusing on breathing and counting. Correct practice can help calm the mind and develop deeper awareness.

Shikantaza, often translated as "just sitting," is a form of meditation that involves open awareness without a specific focus. It requires no particular object of concentration and is considered the purest form of Zazen.

In Shikantaza, you are simply required to sit with open awareness. There is no need to focus on anything in particular. Allow thoughts, feelings, and sensations to arise and pass through your mind without attachment or judgment. The goal is to remain fully present and aware of each moment as it comes and goes, cultivating a state of pure being. This practice helps to develop a deep, non-dual awareness of reality, fostering insight and inner peace.
